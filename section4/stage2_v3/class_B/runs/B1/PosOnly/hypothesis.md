# B1 / PosOnly — Compound-soliton formation mechanism in BKdV

## Best current mechanism hypothesis

The local compound-soliton structure in BKdV is a **transient quasi-equilibrium** that arises from the interplay of three effects, not a globally attracting state. Mechanistically:

1. **Algebraic source (H_alpha, lifetime).** Starting from m_0 = u_0 - v_0^2/2 = 0, the BKdV-S5/S7-deep identity m_t|_{m=0} = (v-1)(6 v v_x + v_xxx) immediately drives m off zero. At very early times the deviation grows linearly, m(x,t) ~ t * S(x), so the locking ratio R = <u, v^2/2> / <(v^2/2)^2> decays from 1 on a timescale **tau_alpha ~ ||v^2/2||_2 / ||S||_2 ~ O(0.5) for A=1.5**. This sets the *lifetime* of the compound phase but says nothing about why it is local rather than global.

2. **Radiative cleansing (H_beta, locality).** During this lifetime, m is generated at every x but the m-modes are governed (to leading order in the v-equation) by the linear dispersion v_xxx whose group velocity c_g(k) = -3 k^2 differs strongly from the compound peak phase speed c_v ~ 6 v_peak ~ 9 (positive). High-k m-modes thus retreat from the compound's frame backward in space; low-k m-modes propagate at small group velocity and lag the compound. As a result the **ratio r_local = ||m||_in_support / ||m||_global decreases monotonically from 0.99 at t=0.01 to 0.45 at t=1.0** (E3, R1) even while ||m||_global itself grows from 0 to 2.4. This is the **precise sense** in which the local compound soliton looks like a Gardner soliton (m≈0 inside support => v-equation reduces algebraically to Gardner v_t + 6 v v_x + (3/2)v^2 v_x + v_xxx = 0 there) while the global picture is not Gardner: m exists, it just sits outside the compound's support and propagates differently from the compound.

3. **Amplitude-controlled basin (H_delta).** The (v-1) factor in S(x) flips sign at v_peak = 1, so the source amplitude is non-monotone in IC amplitude A. Empirically (E2), the locked-phase lifetime peaks near A ~ 0.9 (just below the sign flip) at ~0.85 s and drops to ~0.25 s for both A=0.4 (dispersive, source weak but v decays from radiation before locking can sustain) and A=2.0 (source strong on the soliton core, locking destroyed within 0.2 s). The basin of generic-IC convergence to a compound phase is therefore a *band* 0.5 lesssim A lesssim 1.5; below it the system disperses (kb-kdv-smallAmplitude confirmed), above it fragments into ~10-15 peaks. Shape (sech^2 vs Gaussian vs bimodal) matters only at the second order; in E1, all m_0=0 ICs at A=1.5 show locking R~0.9 at t=0.05, transition to R<0 by t=0.75-1.0, and arrive at ~10 peaks at T=10.

Equivalence with Gardner. Inside the compound's instantaneous support and during t < tau_alpha (~ 0.5 s for A=1.5), the BKdV v-equation reduces dynamically to the Gardner equation up to the m·v coupling which contributes only at O(t^2). The compound velocity *during the locked phase* tracks the order-of-magnitude Gardner prediction c_G ~ 6 v_peak + 1.5 v_peak^2 (~12 at v=1.5, ~6.5 at v=0.9), and v_peak decays as the soliton sheds amplitude into radiation. Beyond tau_alpha, m inside support has grown to be comparable to v^2/2 (R_locking < 0.5), the algebraic reduction fails, and the (u, v) pair fragments into a non-equivalent dispersive multi-peak field. The "global breakdown" of the Gardner equivalence is therefore not an exotic instability — it is the simple consequence of the m=0 manifold being repelling (BKdV-S7-r2, ||m||_L2 reaches 2.55 at saturation in their data, 2.91 in ours at T=2).

## Supporting evidence (from your experiments)

- From E1 / F1: across 6 distinct ICs at A=1.5 (sech^2 m0=0, Gaussian m0=0, sech^2 u0=0, sech^2 A=0.4, bimodal m0=0, bore+v with nu_u=5e-2), every m_0=0 IC shows locking R~0.93 at t=0.05 -> ~0.46 at t=0.5 -> negative by t=1.0; n_peaks grows from 1 to ~10-15 at T=10. The small-amp IC (A=0.4) disperses without ever re-locking (kb-kdv-smallAmplitude confirmed in BKdV setting). The bore IC (nonzero m_0) behaves qualitatively differently, with R staying around 3-5 throughout — this is consistent because the locking ratio R measures u vs v^2/2 alignment, and the bore u sets a nonzero offset.

- From E2 / F2: amplitude sweep A in {0.4, 0.6, 0.9, 1.2, 1.5, 2.0} sech^2 m_0=0 with dense early-time sampling shows the **locked-phase duration is NON-MONOTONE in A**, peaking near A=0.9 at ~0.85 s and dropping symmetrically on both sides. This directly reflects the BKdV-S7-deep loglog slope-0.22 (A<1) vs slope-2.49 (A>=1) prediction: the (v-1) factor in S(x) makes A=1 a sign-flip threshold. Convergence: Nx=256 dt=1e-4 vs Nx=512 dt=2.5e-5 agree to <5% on all diagnostics through t=2 — the fragmentation is physical, not numerical. (Nx=512 with the original dt=1e-4 blew up at t=0.025; bug-fix rerun.)

- From E3 / F3: at fixed A=1.5 sech^2 m_0=0 over T=2, three runs (no viscosity / nu_u=5e-2 / Gardner-shape IC) all collapse v_max similarly (1.50 -> 0.73, 0.75, 0.61) and show R_locking falling through 0.5 at t~0.45. Decisive: **r_local = ||m||_in / ||m||_global decreases monotonically 0.99 -> 0.83 -> 0.67 -> 0.57 -> 0.45 over t in [0.01, 1.0]** even though ||m||_global grows from 0 to 2.4. R_global rises through 1.0 around t=0.25 (overshoot to 1.25 at t=0.5) while R_local is still 0.78 then 0.46. Viscosity makes a ~3% difference in m_in; Gardner-shape IC actually has a SHORTER locked phase than sech^2. The mechanism is therefore neither viscosity-driven nor IC-shape-driven; it is dispersion-mismatch-driven (m-modes flow at group vel -3k^2, compound at phase vel ~6 v_peak).

## Hypotheses considered and falsified / weakened (or shown trivial)

- **H_gamma (Gardner-IC selection)** — TESTED in E3 / R3 via approximate Gardner-shape v_0 = A sech^2 / (1 + delta tanh^2) with delta=A/(A+4), u_0 = v_0^2/2. Outcome: Gardner-shape IC has SHORTER locked phase (R<0.5 by t=0.3, vs t=0.45 for sech^2) and HIGHER m_in by T=2 (1.77 vs 1.27). **STATUS: FALSIFIED.** The locking phenomenon does not require Gardner shape; sech^2 (which has milder gradient profile) locks slightly longer. (Caveat: our Gardner-shape parametrization is approximate, not an exact Gardner-equation eigenstate; a more careful test would use an exact Gardner soliton constructed via the Hirota tau-function — out of scope for 3 rounds.)

- **Viscosity-driven locking (sub-hypothesis of H_alpha)** — TESTED in E3 / R2 with nu_u=5e-2 globally. Outcome: ~3% reduction in m_in at t=0.5 (0.871 vs 0.896), negligible extension of locked phase. **STATUS: WEAKENED.** Viscosity is a stabilizer but is not the mechanism; the local equivalence works perfectly well without viscosity.

- **"Compound soliton persists at T=10" (naive reading of the empirical premise)** — TESTED implicitly by all six E1 ICs and the E2 sweep. Outcome: NO m_0=0 IC at A in [0.4, 2.0] sech^2 produces a persistent compound; every case fragments into ~10-15 peaks by T=10. **STATUS: FALSIFIED for our parameter regime.** The empirical observation must be re-read as "local equivalence during a transient phase," not "persistent locked structure." This is the most important correction: the premise *as stated in the task prompt* is too strong.

- **H_alpha (algebraic-source-dominated lifetime alone)** — TESTED indirectly via the E2 A-sweep showing the locked-phase lifetime varies as predicted by the source S(x), and via E3 showing that viscosity (which damps the v_xxx part of S) has negligible effect. **STATUS: PARTIALLY TRUE.** H_alpha correctly predicts lifetime tau_alpha but does NOT explain locality (Gardner-equivalence holds LOCALLY, not globally). The full picture requires H_alpha + H_beta.

## Open questions / what 1 more experiment would test

A 4th round would target three open questions:

1. **Direct measurement of m-radiation flux out of compound support.** Compute the flux integral d/dt [int_support m^2 dx] = -int_boundary (m^2 * c_g) dx and check it matches the observed decay of r_local. This would *quantitatively* confirm H_beta by showing the m-mass leaving the support equals the rate at which r_local drops. Implementation: integrate m^2 inside the moving compound support, take its time derivative, compare to flux. Bank citation would be the BKdV-S5/S7-deep identity, generalized to a flux form.

2. **True Gardner-soliton IC test.** Construct a numerically exact Gardner single-soliton via Hirota or numerical relaxation (within the v_t + 6 v v_x + (3/2)v^2 v_x + v_xxx = 0 equation), use it as v_0 with u_0 = v_0^2/2 in full BKdV, and measure whether the locked phase is now substantially longer than our approximate Gardner-shape (which falsified the H_gamma sub-hypothesis). If still short, H_gamma is conclusively dead.

3. **Domain-size sensitivity (radiative-sink test).** Repeat E3-R1 at L = 60, 120 with all else equal. If r_local drops more steeply on larger domains, that is positive evidence that the periodic boundary recycles radiation back through the compound on L=30; if r_local is unchanged, the radiation does not significantly re-enter the compound and our conclusions are insensitive to L. This is the cleanest single-experiment test of whether the L=30 results extrapolate to whole real-line BKdV.
