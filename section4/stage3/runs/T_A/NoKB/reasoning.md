## Final method (E3)

Direct (u, N, psi) Fourier pseudospectral on periodic x in [-15, 15], Nx = 256, with:
- Explicit RK4 time stepping, dt = 1e-3
- Periodic-compatible representation: psi = phi - v*x with v = 0.5, so psi(x, 0) = 0; phi_x = psi_x + v
- N-floor regularization in the quantum potential: sqrt(N + eps_N) with eps_N = 1e-6
- Direct (u, N, psi) advancement, with u_t derived from m_t and the algebraic relation m = u - N*phi_x:
  u_t = m_t + N_t * phi_x + N * psi_{xt}

Outputs phi by reconstruction phi = psi + v*x.

Result: the explicit Fourier-RK4 method blows up at step 2 (t = 2e-3), reaching |u| ~ 1e17 and N ~ 1e10 well before T = 8.0. The phenomenon target (soliton survival, m small, bounded fields, < 5% mass drift) is NOT met.

## Iteration trace

### E1 — Direct (u, N, phi) Fourier pseudospectral + RK4, naive (eps=1e-12 in Q)

Hypothesis: the simplest meaningful baseline is direct (u, N, phi) advancement with Fourier pseudospectral + RK4.

Result: blow-up at step 1; final diagnostics all O(1e10) or worse. NaN-free up to that point. F1 initially attributed failure to 1/sqrt(N) amplification of round-off in the soliton tails (N_min ~ 1e-25); a closer probe revealed the **dominant** issue is the non-periodic IC phi(x, 0) = 0.5 * x, which produces Gibbs oscillations of amplitude ~ 80 in the Fourier-derivative phi_x at the boundary (vs the correct 0.5 in the interior). Both issues are recorded in F1.

### E2 — Same method + N-floor regularization eps_N = 1e-6

Single change vs E1: regularize Q = sqrt(N + eps_N)_{xx} / (2 sqrt(N + eps_N)) with eps_N = 1e-6.

Result: still blows up at step 1. F2 confirms N-floor alone cannot fix the non-periodic phi failure mode; the Gibbs spikes in phi_x dominate. Decision: switch the phi representation in E3.

### E3 — E2 + periodic-compatible representation psi = phi - v*x

Single change vs E2: track psi instead of phi, where psi = phi - 0.5*x and psi(x, 0) = 0 is genuinely periodic. phi_x = psi_x + 0.5 everywhere. The PDE rewritten in psi has identical structure (phi only appears through phi_x).

Result: representation fix worked — step 0 RHS values are physical (k1u peak ~ 9, k1N peak ~ 5). But after one full RK4 step, u jumps from peak 1.13 to ~ 256 (200x amplification in dt = 1e-3), and by step 2 the system reaches |u| ~ 1e17. F3 diagnoses this as a fundamental property of the +Q sign convention: the user's variational form has sqrt(N)_xx / (2 sqrt(N)) with positive sign, which is OPPOSITE to standard NLS Madelung. This anti-Bohm sign makes the linearized phase equation contain a backward-heat (anti-diffusion) operator at high k. High-k modes generated by nonlinear products (u*m, (u+phi_x)*N, phi_x^2) are amplified by ~ exp(+k^2 dt / 2) each step, immediately triggering nonlinear blow-up of the explicit scheme.

## Final self-assessment

**useful_self_assessment = FALSE.** The phenomenon target is not met. All three explicit Fourier-RK4 attempts blow up well before T = 8.0:
- E1, E2: blow-up at step 1 (t = 1e-3) due to non-periodic phi IC
- E3: blow-up at step 2 (t = 2e-3) due to anti-diffusive +Q sign at high k

The signal is informative for the research graph:
1. The B-NLS phase IC is rarely periodic; representations should always factor out a constant phi_x boost (psi = phi - v*x).
2. The +Q sign convention of the user's variational form is the **load-bearing structural obstacle** for explicit time-stepping. Stable schemes must either:
   - Use IMEX with the +Q (anti-diffusive) part treated implicitly so it acts as backward Euler damping
   - Apply aggressive dealiasing (2/3 rule) plus spectral hyperviscosity to suppress the high-k modes that the anti-diffusion would amplify
   - Switch to a Lagrangian/characteristic-following formulation that avoids Eulerian high-k aliasing

Future E4-and-beyond direction (out of scope for this 3-iteration session): the most natural single upgrade from E3 would be exponential time differencing (ETD) or IMEX-BDF2 with the +Q-anti-diffusive part treated implicitly — equivalent to a backward Euler "smoothing" of high-k content that compensates for the wrong-sign quantum pressure.
